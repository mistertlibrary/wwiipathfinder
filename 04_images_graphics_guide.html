🖼️ Finding Images & Graphics

Your handout needs at least 3 high-quality, properly cited images

📋 Assignment Requirements

  • Minimum 3 images or graphics on your handout
  • Images must be relevant to your topic
  • Images must be properly cited (see below)
  • Use high-quality images that will print clearly
  • Mix image types: maps, photographs, political cartoons, artwork, diagrams

🌐 Where to Find Copyright-Free Images

All of these sources provide images you can legally use in your handout:

Wikimedia Commons

The go-to source for historical images. Massive collection of photographs, maps, political cartoons, and artwork. All images are public domain or openly licensed.

🎯 Quick Search Tips

Be Specific

Instead of "Africa colonialism," try "Berlin Conference map 1884" or "King Leopold Congo photograph"

Check Image Size

Choose images at least 1000px wide for clear printing. Look for file size info in image details.

Use Filters

Most databases let you filter by type (photograph, map, etc.), date range, and usage rights.

Read Captions

Original captions tell you what you're looking at and often provide citation info you'll need.

⚠️ CITATION IS REQUIRED

You MUST cite every image on your handout. This shows academic integrity and helps classmates find the original source.

What to include in your citation:

  • Title or description of image
  • Creator (photographer, artist, cartoonist) if known
  • Date (when image was created)
  • Source (where you found it)
Example Citation:
"Map of Africa Showing Colonial Holdings, 1914." Library of Congress Geography and Map Division. https://www.loc.gov/item/2002622892/
Another Example:
Political cartoon by John Tenniel, "The Rhodes Colossus," Punch Magazine, December 10, 1892. Via Wikimedia Commons.
