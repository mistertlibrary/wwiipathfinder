Open Web Resources

Free, high-quality sources for researching 19th-century imperialism

What is this? "Open web" resources are freely accessible online without paywalls, subscriptions, or login requirements. These are reliable sources curated specifically for your imperialism peer teaching topics.

How to use: Start with the "Essential" sources for general research, then explore topic-specific collections based on your chosen subject.

📜 Primary Source Collections

Historical documents, letters, treaties, and first-hand accounts from the imperial era

⭐ Essential

Internet History Sourcebooks

Fordham University's curated primary source collection. Check the "Modern History" section for imperialism documents with helpful context. Includes treaties, speeches, and eyewitness accounts.

Visit Sourcebooks →
⭐ Essential

Europeana

50+ million digitized items from European cultural institutions. Search for documents, photographs, and artifacts from all colonial powers. Excellent for finding visual primary sources.

Visit Europeana →
Colonial Era

British Library Digital Collections

Digitized colonial documents, maps, and photographs from the British Empire. The Endangered Archives Programme includes materials from colonized regions in Asia and Africa.

Visit BL Collections →
Global

World Digital Library

UNESCO project providing primary sources from around the world. Includes manuscripts, photographs, and documents from Africa, Asia, Middle East, and Latin America during the imperial period.

Visit WDL →
General

Internet Archive

Massive digital library with 19th-century books, pamphlets, and periodicals. Great for finding full texts of treaties, speeches, travel narratives, and contemporary accounts.

Visit Archive →
Full Texts

Google Books

Search full-text 19th-century publications. Many imperialism-era texts are fully viewable. Use date filters (1800-1914) to find period sources.

Visit Google Books →

🖼️ Visual & Image Sources

Maps, photographs, political cartoons, and artwork for your handouts and presentations

Best Images

Wikimedia Commons

The go-to source for historical images. Massive collection of public domain photographs, maps, political cartoons, and artwork from the imperial era. All freely usable.

Visit Commons →
Maps

Library of Congress Maps

Incredible historical map collection. Search for colonial territory maps, trade routes, and territorial expansion. High-resolution downloads available.

Visit LOC Maps →
Photos

NYPL Digital Collections

New York Public Library's 900,000+ digitized items include extensive imperialism-era photographs, illustrations, and maps. Strong collection on colonial Africa and Asia.

Visit NYPL →
All Types

Library of Congress Prints & Photographs

Historical photographs, political cartoons, posters, and prints. Excellent for finding visual perspectives on imperialism from multiple viewpoints.

Visit LOC Photos →

📚 Scholarly Platforms

Academic resources for background reading and historiography

Directory organized by country, providing links to digitized primary sources, government documents, and historical texts from across Europe and its colonies.
